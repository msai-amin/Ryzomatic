## üìö Scenario 1: **Theoretical Framework Mapper**
### *"Connect the Dots Across Philosophical Traditions"*

**The Challenge:**
Humanities scholars often struggle to identify how different theoretical frameworks intersect across texts, disciplines, and time periods. A paper on post-structuralism might relate to earlier phenomenology, but the connections aren't always explicit.

**The Solution:**
The AI assistant automatically identifies theoretical frameworks, philosophical traditions, and methodological approaches in the PDF, then maps them to:
- **Historical lineage**: "This argument echoes Foucault's 'archaeology of knowledge' (1969), which builds on Nietzsche's genealogical method (1887)"
- **Cross-disciplinary connections**: "The author's use of 'liminality' relates to Turner's anthropological work (1969) and Bhabha's postcolonial theory (1994)"
- **Contemporary debates**: "This critique engages with the 'material turn' in feminist philosophy (Barad, 2007; Coole & Frost, 2010)"

**Features:**
- **Visual Knowledge Map**: Interactive diagram showing theoretical connections
- **Timeline View**: Chronological evolution of ideas from ancient to contemporary
- **Discipline Bridge**: Identifies where humanities disciplines intersect (e.g., philosophy ‚Üí sociology ‚Üí literary theory)
- **Debate Tracker**: Shows where the text positions itself in ongoing academic debates

**User Journey:**
```
1. Upload PDF: "Derrida's Reading of Heidegger"
2. AI Analysis: Identifies key concepts (diff√©rance, Being, deconstruction)
3. Framework Map: Shows connections to:
   - Heidegger's phenomenology ‚Üí Derrida's deconstruction ‚Üí Butler's gender performativity
   - Linguistic turn ‚Üí Post-structuralism ‚Üí Posthumanism

## üîç Scenario 2: **Close Reading Companion**
### *"AI-Powered Textual Analysis for Literary Scholars"*

**The Challenge:**
Close reading requires meticulous attention to language, rhetoric, metaphor, and intertextuality. Scholars spend hours manually annotating texts, tracking recurring motifs, and identifying stylistic patterns.

**The Solution:**
The AI performs multi-layered textual analysis that highlights:
- **Rhetorical devices**: Identifies metaphors, metonymy, synecdoche, irony, and their significance
- **Intertextual references**: Detects allusions, citations, and dialogues with other texts
- **Narrative structure**: Maps plot arcs, focalization shifts, and temporal disruptions
- **Language patterns**: Analyzes diction, syntax, tone shifts, and semantic fields
- **Symbolic networks**: Tracks recurring symbols, motifs, and thematic clusters

**Features:**
- **Layer-by-Layer Analysis**: Toggle between different analytical lenses (formalist, psychoanalytic, feminist, postcolonial)
- **Comparative Reading**: Side-by-side analysis of multiple texts showing parallel structures or contrasting approaches
- **Annotation Assistant**: Suggests relevant critical theory for specific passages
- **Quote Bank**: Automatically extracts and categorizes key quotations with page numbers

**User Journey:**
```
1. Upload PDF: Virginia Woolf's "To the Lighthouse"
2. Select Analysis: "Modernist narrative techniques + Feminist reading"
3. AI Highlights:
   - Stream of consciousness patterns in Mrs. Ramsay's sections
   - Symbolic significance of the lighthouse (desire, absence, memory)
   - Gender dynamics in the dinner party scene
   - Intertextual connections to Joyce's "Ulysses" and Eliot's "The Waste Land"
4. Generate Notes: "The lighthouse functions as a Lacanian objet petit a, 
   representing the unattainable object of desire..."
```

### *"Situate Any Text in Its Socio-Historical Moment"*

**The Challenge:**

**The Solution:**
The AI instantly generates rich historical context for any text, including:
- **Biographical context**: Author's life, influences, and intellectual trajectory
- **Historical moment**: Major political, social, and cultural events during writing
- **Intellectual milieu**: Dominant schools of thought, key debates, and influential figures
- **Publication history**: Reception, translations, and scholarly impact over time
- **Archival connections**: Links to primary sources, letters, and contemporary documents

**Features:**
- **Timeline Integration**: Places the text on an interactive historical timeline
- **Cultural Encyclopedia**: Explains period-specific references, terminology, and concepts
- **Contemporary Voices**: Shows what other scholars were writing at the same time
- **Reception History**: Tracks how the text has been interpreted across decades
- **Archive Finder**: Suggests relevant archival collections and primary sources

**User Journey:**
```

## üí° Scenario 4: **Argument Reconstruction Assistant**
### *"Decode Complex Philosophical & Critical Arguments"*

**The Challenge:**
Humanities texts often present layered, non-linear arguments that are difficult to follow. Philosophers and critical theorists may take 50 pages to build a single argument, with multiple digressions and qualifications.

**The Solution:**
The AI reconstructs arguments in clear, logical steps, showing:
- **Main thesis**: Identifies the central claim(s) of the text
- **Sub-arguments**: Breaks down supporting arguments and their relationships
- **Evidence**: Catalogues examples, case studies, and empirical support
- **Counter-arguments**: Identifies objections the author addresses
- **Logical structure**: Maps premises, inferences, and conclusions
- **Assumptions**: Reveals implicit philosophical or methodological assumptions

**Features:**
- **Argument Flowchart**: Visual diagram of the argumentative structure
- **Premise Checker**: Evaluates the strength of key premises
- **Objection Generator**: Suggests potential critiques from different theoretical perspectives
- **Simplified Summary**: Explains complex arguments in accessible language
- **Citation Network**: Shows how the argument builds on previous scholarship

**User Journey:**
```
1. Upload PDF: Judith Butler's "Gender Trouble" (Chapter 1)
2. AI Reconstruction:
   Main Argument: Gender is performatively constituted through repeated acts
   
   Premise 1: Sex/gender distinction assumes a natural body (critique of feminism)
   Premise 2: Identity categories are regulatory fictions (Foucault + Nietzsche)
   Premise 3: Performativity ‚â† performance (Austin's speech act theory)
   Conclusion: Gender is produced through stylized repetition of acts
   
   Key Assumptions: 
   - Language is constitutive, not descriptive
   - Power operates through discourse (Foucault)
   - Identity is temporal and unstable
   
3. Implications: "This challenges biological essentialism and opens space 
   for queer theory and transgender studies"
```

- **Synthesis Opportunities**: Suggests productive combinations of theoretical frameworks
- **Research Questions**: Generates provocative, publishable research questions
- **Methodological Innovation**: Proposes new ways to approach old questions
- **Cross-Pollination**: Suggests insights from adjacent disciplines

**Features:**
- **Literature Review Assistant**: Summarizes the current state of a debate across multiple texts
- **Debate Matrix**: Compares how different scholars approach the same question
- **Innovation Radar**: Identifies emerging trends and under-explored areas
- **Writing Prompts**: Generates thesis statements and argument sketches
- **Citation Suggestions**: Recommends additional texts to strengthen the argument

**User Journey:**
